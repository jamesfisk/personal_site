Dating Shakespeare's Sonnets

Very little historical information survives surrounding Shakespeare's composition of his 1609 Sonnet Sequence. The first evidence of some Sonnets appears in 1598, but when he wrote the rest of the sequence, and in what order, over the following decade is almost completely unknown. I have been working with stylometric and data mining techniques to help shed light on this vexing question. Using various methods of clustering and classifying, including k-means, Bayesian and Artificial Neural Network classifiers, I have helped to discover what the chronological evidence within the Sonnets themselves. My senior thesis offers a comprehensive report of my work so far.




Oompa

Working as a 2015 intern for Target headquarters on their big data team, I helped to build a Grails web application to monitor Target's Hadoop big data cluster. The application, named Oompa, regularly queried the cluster's API for its status, stored it in a MySQL database, and exposed both current performance and overall trends through D3-generated visualizations. I contributed to the full stack of the application, working closely with the Hadoop platform as well as constructing the end-user interface.
